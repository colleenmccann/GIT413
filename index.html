About Me

Something for Everyone (GIT230)

This was an early student work for a design course I took before deciding to take the web development route in my degree. Nintendo often focuses on being a family-friendly video game company, but that can alienate people who play more mature video games. This advertisement features several different games of different genres and ESRB ratings (E for Everyone, E10+ for Everyone aged 10 and up, T for Teen, and M for Mature) to convey the message that every gamer has something to play on the Nintendo Switch.

Claire Sinagra (GIT450)

Claire Sinagra needed a responsive web design to fit several different device sizes. A mobile-first approach to web design and development allows for better search engine optimization as well as a more user-friendly experience. Users on any device will have an easy time navigating through different areas of the site-media queries are used so that the site will adapt to a mobile, tablet, or desktop design based on the user's method of access.

Ced's Succulents (GIT417)

This is a website focused on showcasing different functions of JavaScript. It's themed around my favorite types of succulents. JavaScript features are shown with event listeners for a dark mode toggle button, a random number match game, and a carousel menu with different types of succulents shown. This was one of my favorite projects so far because I was given a lot of creative freedom! I had lots of fun choosing the color scheme and tone of the site.

Calculus Kept Simple (Senior Project)

A lot of students struggle with calculus, which gets very complex very quickly. Confusion with complex concepts in calculus often stems from a lack of proper understanding of fundamental concepts, but a lot of math tutorials overcomplicate things, causing frustration. This site breaks numbers down into words, but keeps explanations concise. It's a tried-and-true method I used for years when tutoring my friends in calculus. The site itself is responsive, supporting mobile, tablet, and desktop layouts, and is multi-page.

lo-fi | life's bgm (GIT337)

A one-page concept site meant as practice with HTML and CSS—but it features a working music player (media embed) with my favorite lo-fi song! Muted colors fit right in with the calming tone that lo-fi creates. I'd like to revisit this sometime by either incorporating its themes into a future website design or finishing the page as a personal project. The site is partially responsive, having only one layout but adjusting sizes of images and spacing based on the browser size.
